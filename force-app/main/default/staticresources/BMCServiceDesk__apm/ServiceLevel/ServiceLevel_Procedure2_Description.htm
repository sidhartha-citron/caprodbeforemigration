Procedure 2, Service Activation


If the task for the Service Level Management requests the registration of a new service instance, the service level administrator fills the Service form in the service management application.

If the task requests the preparation of a new SLA, the service level administrator prepares a new SLA by filling out the Service Level Agreement Template, printing two copies, and attaching a printout of the relevant catalog item to each copy. The service level administrator then collects the signatures of both the customer representative and the service owner who are responsible for meeting the SLTs specified in the SLA.

If either the customer representative or the service owner decided not to sign the SLA, the service level administrator goes to Procedure 4, Service Termination to mention the reason of not establishing the new SLA in the task. If the new SLA was signed, the service level administrator files one copy of the signed SLA and returns the other to the customer representative for their files.

Before registering a new SLA, the service level administrator checks the service management application to see if the contact details of the customer are up to date. If the customer of the SLA has not yet been registered, or if the contact details of the customer are no longer up to date, the service level administrator ensures that the customer information is registered or updated in Procedure 3, Customer Information Maintenance.

When the contact details of the customer are latest, the service level administrator registers the new SLA in the service management application and links it to the service instance that is used to provide the service to the customer.
