Roles and responsibilities


The table below lists the different roles that are involved in the Service Level Management process, along with their respective responsibilities. Click a role to review its profile.

Role

Responsibility

Service Level Administrator

  • Maintains the web-based service catalog of the service provider organization by adding and removing catalog items as requested by Change Management

  • Prepares and gets the sign off for SLAs as requested by Change Management

  • Registers services and SLAs in the service management application to ensure that this information is available to the rest of the service provider organization

  • Registers the contact details of individual customers, customer organizations, and customer representatives when new customers subscribe to the service of the service provider organization

  • Updates the information about individual customers, customer organizations, and customer representatives after updated contact information has been received

Service Level Manager

  • Produces reports to compare actual versus agreed upon service levels at the end of an SLA evaluation term

  • Finds out for each SLT that was violated during an SLA evaluation term, why it was violated and which actions the service owner has taken, or plans to take, to ensure that the SLT is not be violated again

  • Proactively adds improvement suggestions to the SLA evaluation report for customer representatives

  • Reviews actual versus agreed upon service levels with the customer representative after the end of an SLA evaluation term

  • Registers the requests from customer representatives as support requests in the service management application and passes the requests to the service desk

  • Writes and distributes the notes of the SLA review meetings.
