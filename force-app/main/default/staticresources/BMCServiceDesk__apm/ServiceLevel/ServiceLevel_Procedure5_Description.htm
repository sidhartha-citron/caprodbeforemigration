Procedure 5, SLA Review and Request Handling


When the SLAs of one of the customer organization of the service level manager have reached the end of their evaluation term, the service level manager schedules a meeting with the representative for this customer. Next, the service level manager determines the actual level at which the services have been provided over the past term to the customer of the SLAs.

The service level manager subsequently compares the actual service levels with the service level targets (SLTs) specified in the SLAs. If one or more SLTs are violated during the evaluation term, the service level manager contacts the responsible service owners to find out why the SLTs are violated and how the service owners can ensure that these SLTs are not violated again. The service level manager uses all this information, along with their proactive improvement suggestions, to compile a detailed report for the customer representative.

The service level manager reviews the report with the customer representative, preferably within one month after the end of the evaluation term. During the meeting the customer representative informs the service level managers of any requests from the customer organization that they represent.

After the meeting, the requests are registered as requests by the service level manager and passed to the most appropriate group. The service level manager then documents the minutes of the meeting and references the numbers of these requests. The meeting notes are distributed to the customer representative, concerned service owners, and service desk manager.

Ad hoc requests from customer representatives are also registered by the service level manager and passed to the most appropriate group.
