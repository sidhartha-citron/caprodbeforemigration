Procedure 4, Service Termination


If the task for the Service Level Management requests the discontinuation of a catalog item, the service level administrator ensures that it can no longer be accessed by customers from the web-based service catalog of the service provider organization.

If the task requests the termination of one or more SLAs, the service level administrator updates the status of these SLAs to indicate that the SLAs have been discontinued. In addition, the service level administrator checks the status of the other SLAs that are linked to the services which SLAs the administrator has discontinued. If all SLAs linked to these services are now discontinued, the service level administrator also discontinues these services by updating their status.

When no further action is required from the service level administrator to update the Service Level Management information, the administrator updates the task to inform the success or failure to the change coordinator (for example, the requested catalog item was not approved or the requested SLA was not signed).
