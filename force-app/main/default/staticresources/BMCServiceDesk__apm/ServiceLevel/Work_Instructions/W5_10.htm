Work Instructions for Service Level Managers

5.10.1

Discuss each request with the customer representative to ensure that you understand the customer’s business requirements. To understand the requirement of the service provider, create a concise set of SLRs when the customer representative asks for a new service or a service to be provided at a different level.

Note:

To translate the customer's business requirements into SLRs, refer the catalog item that the customer has selected. But if the customer request a new service, you may need to assist the representative to decide on the required level of support, availability, reliability, and so on. The Continuity Risk Assessment Scorecard can be used to help the customer representative decide on the appropriate level of continuity coverage for the service.

Note:

If the customer representative submits a complaint, it is important to understand the impact on the customer's business. Use the information to avoid misunderstandings within the service provider organization.

Note:

Customer representatives should report incidents directly to the service desk. When the customer representative requests for change, ask him to use the web request form to submit the request for change. Such support requests should not be handled by service level managers.

Note:

Register the request only after the business requirements are clarified.
