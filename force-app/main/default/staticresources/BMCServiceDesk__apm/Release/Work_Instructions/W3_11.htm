Work Instructions for Release Coordinators

3.11.1

Attach the rejected business case to the business justification task, and in its Resolution field, specify why it was rejected.

3.11.2

Set the Status field of the business justification task to Failed.

In the Duration (HH:MM) field, in the Notes action, enter the time that you spent updating the task.

3.11.3

Open the task for updating the requesters and approvers of the release. This task is linked to the same change as the business justification task and was also generated by the change template when this change was registered.

3.11.4

Update the Due Date field of the task by specifying a date and time that can be achieved.

3.11.5

Use the Change option next to the Owner field to select yourself in the Owner field of the task.

3.11.6

If you are not ready to update the requester and approver, change the Status field of the task from Registered to Accepted. When you are ready to update the requester and approver, set the Status field of the task to In Progress.

3.11.7

View the change requests of the rejected release in the Linked Incidents and Linked Problems sections of the change that is used for the coordination of the release (this is also the change to which the work order for updating the requesters and approvers of the release is linked).

3.11.8

From these sections, open each request and problem. In a support request, in its Action History section, specify that the business case for the release to which it was linked has been rejected. Also, include the reason for the rejection and specify that the change request will be reviewed again during the next CAB meeting for the service. Link the placeholder change of the service in the Linked Change Requests section of the request. Similarly, update the linked problems. To link the problem back to the placeholder change in the Linked Change Requests section, click the Select and Link Change Requests to Release.

3.11.9

Remove the link between each rejected change request and the change that is used for the coordination of the release. To remove a link with a request, in the Linked Incidents section of the change, click Del in the Action column. To remove a link with a problem, in the Linked Problems section of the change, click Del in the Action column.

3.11.10

Send an email to the requester of each change request (that is support request or problem) that you moved back to the change that acts as a placeholder for requests that are waiting to be reviewed during the next CAB meeting of the service. In each email, include the information that you entered in the Information update field of the change request of the email recipient.

3.11.11

Send a similar email to the approvers of the rejected business case to inform them that the release has been rejected and is closed.
