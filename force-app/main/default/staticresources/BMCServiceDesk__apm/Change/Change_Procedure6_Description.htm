Procedure 6, Planned Change Closure

After the change has been put into production, a specialist (with the help of a user, if the specialist does not have sufficient access rights) performs a test to verify the success of the implementation.

If the specialist determines that all the requirements of the change have been met, the CMDB, the Capacity Management, and the Continuity Management information is updated as needed. The change coordinator subsequently informs the requesters and approvers of the change that the change has been implemented successfully. Then, the change coordinator closes the change request.

If all the requirements have not been met, however, the specialist who performed the production test determines whether the change must be rolled back. If the change does not provide an improvement over the previous situation or causes a security or data integrity risk, the change is rolled back.

If the implementation can still be successful, the change coordinator creates the necessary additional tasks. However, if it is not clear how all change requirements can be met, the change coordinator informs the requesters and approvers of the situation and closes the change request.
