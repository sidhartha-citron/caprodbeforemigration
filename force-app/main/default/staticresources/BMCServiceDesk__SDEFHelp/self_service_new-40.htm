Remedyforce Chat BETA is available with Self Service 2.0 and enables clients to chat with a staff member for assistance without having to leave the Self Service site.

You can select staff members who can provide help by using chat in addition to their other tasks. When any of the staff members configured for chat are logged on to Remedyforce Console tab and their status is marked as available to chat, a client request for chat assistance is routed to one of them (in a random order).

Chat module extends the Self Service experience for clients and allows them an option to self-serve or use a real-time assistance. Clients can use chat from Self Service without having to go a new place to access it.

Through a chat conversation, the staff member can:

    • answer client's queries
    • provide help with appropriate service request selections
    • share knowledge article links or help troubleshoot issues by providing steps for resolution

If no staff member is logged on to the Remedyforce Console tab and marked as available for chat, a message about no staff members being available is displayed in the Self Service chat area to the client.

By using the Remedyforce Chat, currently a staff member can assist one client via chat at a given time. A staff member can support another client request for chat only after the chat with the first client has successfully ended. At the end of a chat session, the staff member has an option to create an incident record and save the transcript of the chat in the Description field of the incident.
