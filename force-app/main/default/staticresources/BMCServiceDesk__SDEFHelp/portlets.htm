You can choose to display or hide the following portlets in the Sidebar:

    • Action Items—This portlet allows the logged-on staff member to view the number of assigned incidents, tasks, broadcasts, change requests, and problems. The staff member can assess the amount of work that must be completed and can plan a work schedule for the day. The logged-on staff member can directly access the assigned items by clicking the required item to open the List view of the item. For example, if the logged-on staff member clicks Incidents in this portlet, the Incident List view opens and displays only those incidents that are assigned to the staff member.

      • When you submit a service request, the number of assigned incidents increases by one if the logged-on user is the owner of service request. The number of assigned change requests and tasks does not change even when change requests and tasks are created to fulfill the service request.

      When you create an incident or change request by using a template, the number of assigned incidents or change requests increases by one. However, the number of tasks does not increase if the template has linked tasks.

    • Broadcasts—This portlet allows the logged-on staff member to view the existing broadcasts if the broadcasts are displayed to everyone or to their profile. The staff member can select a broadcast to view the Broadcast Message in the portlet. The staff member can follow the Broadcast to receive updates in their feed.
    • Search—This portlet allows the logged-on staff member to perform a Global Search without having to open Global Search.
    • World Time—This portlet allows the logged-on staff member to view the time in three different parts of the world simultaneously. Staff members can use the clocks in this portlet to assign tasks to their counterparts in different time zones. They can also view the time in different time zones and find out if it is a good time to contact a client because many clients are from different geographic locations.
    • Top Requests—This portlet allows the logged-on staff member to create incidents from Incident templates with a click. When you click an Incident template in this portlet, a new Incident form opens and its fields are filled with values from the Incident template. A template appears in the Top Requests portlet if the template is shared with your profile or with everyone. This portlet displays a maximum of five templates. You can configure the order in which Incident templates are displayed in the portlet by opening the template in the Template form and defining display order in this form. For more information, see Creating templates.

      • Note: The changes made to the template display order reflect in the Top Requests portlet when you refresh your browser or change the display order of portlets in the Sidebar by dragging a portlet upwards or downwards in the Sidebar.

    • Chatter Groups—This portlet allows the logged-on staff member to view the Chatter groups that he or she is a member of. When you click a Chatter group in this portlet, the detail page of the Chatter group opens. The group detail page displays all posts, comments, and links that are shared by the group members. The Chatter Groups portlet does not display in the Sidebar if you have not created Chatter groups in your Salesforce.com organization. For more information about Chatter groups, see https://na11.salesforce.com/help/doc/user_ed.jsp?section=help&target=collab_group_overview.htm&loc=help&hash=topic-title. For more information about using Chatter groups, see Using the Chatter Feed.

      • Note: You can change the display order of portlets in the Sidebar by dragging a portlet upwards or downwards in the Sidebar. Additionally, click Next Portlet or Previous Portlet to view the remaining portlets in the Sidebar.

To configure portlets in the Sidebar

  1. In the BMC Remedyforce home page, click Add in the upper right section.
  2. In the Sidebar Content window, select the portlets that should be displayed in the Sidebar.
  3. Click OK.
  4. In the World Time portlet, click the Settings icon to configure this portlet.
  5. For each clock, perform the following actions:
    1. From the Time Zone list, select time zone whose time you want to display in the clock.

      2. The Time Zone list displays the standard and daylight time zones and codes. When Daylight Savings Time comes in to effect, you must select the appropriate time zone in the Time Zone list to reflect the correct time.

    2. (Optional) Type the name of the city whose time you want to display in the portlet.
    3. Click OK.

      4. The clocks display the time based on the settings you have configured.
