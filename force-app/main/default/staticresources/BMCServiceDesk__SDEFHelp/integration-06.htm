When BMC Remedyforce and BMC Client Management are integrated, staff members can perform the following actions:

    • Launch the BMC Client Management console from the CMDB Manager in BMC Remedyforce.
    • View the asset summary of devices imported from BMC Client Management.
    • Perform BMC Client Management actions on the BMC Client Management device imported in BMC Remedyforce.
    • Create service request definitions (SRDs) and Incident templates that deploy operational rules and make them available to Self Service clients.
    • Attach BMC Client Management operational rules to Task templates to deploy the operational rules on BMC Client Management devices.
    • Search, import, and link a device to an incident or service request.
    • Deploy operational rules on a BMC Client Management device.

      • Note: You are prompted to provide your credentials the first time you log on to the BMC Client Management console. You are not prompted for credentials for future connection attempts unless you change your BMC Client Management password. For more information, see Integrated authentication for BMC Client Management administrators.

If you are integrating with BMC Client Management 11.7, you have the option to enable the enhanced UI. In this case, BMC Remedyforce uses the Representational State Transfer (REST) web API in BMC Client Management to retrieve device data and perform actions on the devices from the CI Actions window. Users do not have to launch the BMC Client Management console to perform these actions. For information about the REST web API in BMC Client Management, see the BMC Client Management Help.
