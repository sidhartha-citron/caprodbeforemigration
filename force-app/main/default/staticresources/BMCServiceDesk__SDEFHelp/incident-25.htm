In a new Incident form, the Incident Chatter Feed is disabled for editing. It is enabled for editing after you save the record.

Note: If feed tracking is not enabled for the Incident object, the Incident Chatter Feed does not appear in the Incident form.

In the Incident Chatter Feed, you can perform the following actions:

    • View updates that are made to the Incident fields that are enabled for feed tracking
    • Comment on the updates that are made to the Incident fields that are enabled for feed tracking
    • Use a comment as a resolution and close the incident
    • Create posts that you want to share with followers of the record
    • Follow and unfollow the incident record. You can follow a maximum of 500 records.

To use the Incident Chatter Feed

  1. In the BMC Remedyforce home page, click Workspaces.
  2. From the list of Workspaces, expand Incident Management.
  3. In the Incident Management list, click Incidents.
  4. In the incidents list view, select an incident that you own or follow by using one of the following methods:
    • Select a field from the Group By list, navigate to the required group, and select the required incident.
    • Type a search string in the Search field, click Search, and select the required incident from the search results.
    • Scroll through the list of incidents, and select the required incident.

      • Note: By default, when you create or own a record, you follow it. For more information, see Automatically following records.

  5. Click Incident Chatter Feed.

    6. Note: The updates made to the Incident fields that are enabled for feed tracking are visible in the Incident Chatter Feed only after you save the record.

  6. To create a post that you want to share with followers of the incident record, perform the following actions:
    1. Type the post that you want to add to the Chatter Feed.
    2. Click Share.

      3. The post appears in the Incident Chatter Feed and in your Chatter Feed. You can view your Chatter Feed by navigating to Workspaces > Chatter > Chatter Feed.

  7. To comment on the updates that are made to the Incident fields, perform the following actions:
    1. Click the comment link below the update that you want to comment.
    2. Type the comment.
    3. Click the Add Comment link.

      4. The comment appears below the update in the Incident Chatter Feed and in your Chatter Feed. You can delete this comment or use the comment as a resolution for the incident and close the incident.

  8. To use your comment as a resolution for the incident and close the incident, perform the following actions:
    1. To create a comment on an existing update in the Incident Chatter Feed, perform step a through step c.
    2. Click the Add Resolution & Close link.

      3. Your comment is added to the Resolution field and the incident is closed. The following post is added to the Incident Chatter Feed:

      <Logged-on user> changed State Open from true to false and changed Incident Resolution and changed Close Date & Time to <current timestamp>.

  9. Perform one of the following actions to follow or unfollow the incident record:
    • In the Incident Chatter Feed, click the Unfollow link to stop following the incident record and stop seeing updates to the incident record in your Chatter Feed.

      • The contents of the Incident Chatter Feed are deleted.

    • In the Incident Chatter Feed, click the Follow link to restart following the incident record and viewing updates to the incident record in your Chatter Feed.

      • The updates that are made after clicking the Follow link are displayed in the Incident Chatter Feed and in your Chatter Feed.

  10. Click Save.

    11. Note: When you open a new form to create a record and you save the record, you cannot navigate to the next or previous record by using the Next Record and Previous Record buttons in the upper-right section of the form. These buttons are disabled in a new form.

Automatically following records

By default, you automatically follow records that meet the following conditions:

    • You have created the record or you own the record.
    • The system administrator has enabled feed tracking for the object of the record.
    • Another user has assigned the record to you and the system administrator has enabled the Owner field on the object of the record for feed tracking.

You can stop following records by navigating to Setup > My Chatter Settings > My Feeds and selecting the Stop automatically following records check box. Even if you have selected the Stop automatically following records check box, you follow an incident record when you change the status of the incident to a status that has a state of Acknowledged.
