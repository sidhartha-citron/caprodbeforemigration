The Chatter Feed is your home page of the Salesforce Chatter application. The Chatter Feed displays your picture and your latest status update. You can view all of your updates in one place. From the Chatter Feed, you can post updates and share these updates with people who follow you, comment on the posts made on records and people that you follow, and share links with people who follow you. The Chatter Feed allows you to track all updates to the following types of records that you follow:

    • Incident records
    • Broadcast records
    • Change request records
    • Configuration item records

These updates appear in the following format:

<Object Name> <Record Number> <User Name> <Action performed on the 
record>

Note: <Object Name> allows you to view the type of record that is described in the update.

When a configuration item is linked to an incident, change request, or problem, this update appears in the Chatter Feed in the following format: 

<User Name> linked <Object Name> <Record Number> to this 
Configuration Item

By default, when you create or own a record, you automatically follow it. When an incident, broadcast, or change request is closed, you can unfollow it from the Chatter Feed.

The Chatter Feed can be used in the following ways to collaborate with your team members:

    • If you have used a comment to add to the resolution of an incident and close the incident, you can look for the comment in the Chatter Feed and share the comment with a team member who is assigned to a similar incident.
    • If you have found a link that is useful to all members of your Chatter group, you can share the link with them through the Chatter Feed.
    • You can start discussions in the Chatter Feed by using comments on a task record that must be completed by your team.
    • If you are working on a critical issue, you can share periodic progress updates with your team in the Chatter Feed instead of opening your email application and sending an email for each progress update.
    • If a team member requires immediate assistance on an incident, the team member can post the query to his or her Chatter Feed and the query appears in your Chatter Feed. You can add your resolution as a comment on the query and share with the team. Other team members can provide alternate resolutions to the query and a repository of resolutions becomes available to the team.

To use the Chatter Feed

  1. In the BMC Remedyforce home page, click Workspaces.
  2. From the list of Workspaces, expand Chatter.
  3. In the Chatter list, click Chatter Feed.
  4. To create a post that you want to share with your followers, perform the following actions:
    1. Type the post that you want to add to the Chatter Feed.
    2. Click Share.

      3. The post appears in your Chatter Feed and the Chatter Feed of your followers.

  5. To comment on the updates that are made to records that you are following, perform the following actions:
    1. Click the comment link below the update that you want to comment.
    2. Type the comment.
    3. Click the Add Comment link.

      4. The comment appears below the update in the Chatter Feed.

  6. In the Chatter Feed, click the Unfollow link to stop following the record and stop seeing updates to the record in your Chatter Feed.

    7. Updates of the record are deleted from the Chatter Feed.

  7. Perform the following actions to add a link to your Chatter Feed:
    1. Click Link at the top of the Chatter Feed.
    2. In the Attach a Link section, type the URL of the link in the Link URL field.
    3. In the Link Name field, type the name of the link.
    4. Click Share.

      5. If you do not type the name of the link in the Link Name field and only type the URL in the Link URL field, the Chatter Feed displays the URL of the link. For example, if you type bmc.com in the Link URL field, the Chatter Feed displays bmc.com. However, if you type the name of the link in the Link Name field and the URL in the Link URL field, the Chatter Feed displays the name of the link. For example, if you type BMC Website in the Link Name field and bmc.com in the Link URL field, the Chatter Feed displays BMC Website.

Automatically following records

By default, you automatically follow records that meet the following conditions:

    • You have created the record or you own the record.
    • The system administrator has enabled feed tracking for the object of the record.
    • Another user has assigned the record to you and the system administrator has enabled the Owner field on the object of the record for feed tracking.

You can stop automatically following records by navigating to Setup > My Chatter Settings > My Feeds and selecting the Stop automatically following records check box. Even if you have selected the Stop automatically following records check box, you automatically follow an incident record when you change the status of the incident to a status that has a state of Acknowledged.

Note: If you have enabled the improved Setup user interface in your Salesforce organization, navigate to My Settings > Chatter > My Feeds. For more information about the improved Setup user interface, see http://help.salesforce.com/apex/HTViewHelpDoc?id=admin_setup_improved.htm&language=en_US.
