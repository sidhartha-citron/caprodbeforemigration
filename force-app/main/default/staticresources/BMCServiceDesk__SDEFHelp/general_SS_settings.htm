The settings that are applicable to Self Service for all the users are provided on the General Self Service Settings page.

To configure Self Service-wide general settings

  1. Click the Remedyforce Administration tab.
  2. On the Home page, click the Configure Self Service tile, and from the menu select General Self Service Settings.
  3. To enable the staff members to chat with clients, select the Enable Remedyforce Chat — BETA check box.

    4. There are additional steps that must be performed to enable the Remedyforce Chat — BETA. For more information, see Enabling Remedyforce Chat—BETA.

  4. To view knowledge articles before Incident templates and service request definitions in Self Service, select the Knowledge articles have precedence over service requests in the layout check box.

    5. If you select this check box, knowledge articles have precedence in the following sections:

    • The Popular Self Help Articles tab appears on the left and the Common Service Requests tab appears on the right in the Self Service home page.
    • When you perform a search, the Self Help Articles tab appears before the Tickets, Service Requests, and Web tabs in the search results.

      • Note: In Self Service, the knowledge articles are sorted based on the count in the Used in Self Service # and Viewed in Self Service # fields of the knowledge articles. The Viewed in Self Service # count increments by 1 each time Self Service users view a knowledge article. The Used in Self Service # count increments by 1 each time Self Service users submit feedback that the article has met their needs.
      The count of the Used in Self Service # field has higher weightage than the Viewed in Self Service # field.

  5. To display suggested knowledge articles and service requests, select the Display suggested knowledge articles and service requests based on client’s services check box.

    6. For more information, see Displaying suggested knowledge articles and service requests in Self Service.

  6. In the Site URL of Self Service field, enter the Self Service site URL.

    7. Note: To provide direct links to various components, ensure that you enter the URL of the Self Service site in this field. For more information, see Providing direct links for Self Service components.

    This URL is used in the following ways:

    • When a staff member shares a link to an incident with a client in an email message. When the client clicks this link, Self Service view of the incident is displayed.
    • When a staff member copies link to a knowledge article in the Resolution field of an incident. The client can use this link to see the knowledge article in Self Service.
  7. Click Save.
  8. In the confirmation dialog box, click OK.
