Procedure 2: Request Assignment


If the request is a complaint or compliment, the service desk analyst assigns the request to the service desk manager.

If request fulfillment can handle a request, the service desk analyst coordinates implementing the request.

The service desk analyst considers all request categories for resolution. If the service desk analyst cannot resolve the request (in terms of skills, access rights, and time restrictions), the service desk analyst assigns the request to the appropriate group.

The coordinator of the group reviews all requests that are assigned to a group by service desk analysts, operators, continuity managers, and users and customer representatives who submit a web request form.

If the group coordinator determines that a request is assigned to the wrong group or the request does not contain all information that should be provided by the service desk analyst, operator, or continuity manager to complete it, the group coordinator rejects the request and specifies the reason for rejection.

Rejected requests are automatically passed to the service desk. A service desk analyst ensures that the missing information is collected and these rejected requests are reassigned to the appropriate group if the service desk cannot resolve them.

After requests are assigned to the correct group, the requests with the required information are passed to the change coordinator of the related service if the requests are for a change whose implementation is coordinated by Change Management. All other requests are assigned to the appropriate specialist of the group (in terms of skills, availability, and access rights).
