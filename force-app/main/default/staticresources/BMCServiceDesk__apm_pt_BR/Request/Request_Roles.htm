Roles and responsibilities


The following table lists the different roles that are involved in the Request Management process and their respective responsibilities. Click a role to review its profile.

Role

Responsibility

Group coordinator

Distributes requests assigned to the group between the specialists after taking into account the skills, availability, and access rights of the specialists

On-duty manager

Assumes the Incident Escalation Handling responsibilities of service owners when they are not available

Service desk analyst

  • Provides a single point of contact for users of the services provided by the service provider organization

  • Obtains the necessary information from users and efficiently, accurately, and completely registers the information in requests

  • Resolves as many of the received requests as possible within the limitations of the service desk analyst access rights and time constraints

  • Assigns requests that cannot be resolved by the service desk to the appropriate group for resolution

  • Monitors the progress of high-impact incidents (for example, service outages that affect multiple users) and informs all concerned people

  • Escalates a high-impact incident to the service owner of the affected service when the incident has not been resolved within the available resolution time defined in the related SLA

  • Informs users of the solutions after their requests are resolved and asks them to verify the solution

Service desk manager

  • Ensures that users are dealt with courteously

  • Handles all support requests of the Complaint and Compliment categories to ensure that users who reported a complaint turn into satisfied users of the service provider organization

Service owner

Decides whether an escalated incident must be resolved by implementing an emergency change, by recovering the affected service at its continuity site, or by continuing the resolution of the incident within the Request Management process

Specialist

  • Resolves requests and tasks

  • Updates requests and tasks with relevant information and status changes

  • Escalates incidents to the service owner of the affected service for incidents whose resolution can only be implemented through the Change Management process
