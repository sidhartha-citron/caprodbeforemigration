Procedure 7: Incident Escalation Handling


After a specialist or a service desk analyst has escalated an incident, the owner of the affected service talks to the specialists who have been dealing with the incident to understand the current situation and determine how the incident can be resolved.

If the incident is a major incident, the service owner activates the Major Incident team. The Major Incident team leader is responsible for coordinating the resolution.

If the recovery of the affected service at its continuity site is the most efficient and reliable way to resolve the incident, the service owner escalates the incident to the on-duty manager to start the service recovery.

In most cases, the recovery of a service at its continuity site is not going to fix the incident (for example, when a bug causes the incident). Implementing a fix in the service's current infrastructure might be more efficient and reliable. In such cases, the service owner determines if Change Management must coordinate the resolution of the incident. Change Management is required when the resolution of the incident causes:

  • Service to become unavailable or degraded during service hours
  • Functionality of a service to become different
  • CMDB to require an update

If Change Management is not required, the service owner ensures that the most appropriate specialists continue to resolve the incident within the Request Management process.

If Change Management is required, the service owner consults with the specialists to understand the risks that could cause the implementation of the change to fail and the impact of the implementation on customers. They figure out the best way to keep the risk and impact of the change implementation at an acceptable level. After they have established how the change should be implemented, the service owner asks the specialists to perform the implementation as an emergency change.

The on-duty manager performs the role of service owner when the service owner of the affected service is not available.
