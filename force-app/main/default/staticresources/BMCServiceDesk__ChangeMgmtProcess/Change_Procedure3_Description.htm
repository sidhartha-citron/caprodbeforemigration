Description


After the risk and impact analysis has been completed and the implementation plan has been developed, the change manager reviews the change. If the change conflicts with internal standards or policies, the change manager informs the change coordinator that the change cannot be implemented.

If the change does not conflict with internal standards or policies, the change manager reviews the risk and impact analysis and the implementation plan. The change manager verifies that appropriate precautions have been planned to minimize the risk of failure and the impact on customers, and that the timing of the implementation does not conflict with other planned changes or planned events.

If the risk and impact analysis is insufficient, the change manager requests additional analysis from the change coordinator. If the planning does not adequately address the risk of failure or the impact on the customers, or if the planning conflicts with other planned changes or events, the change manager requests an adjustment of the implementation plan from the change coordinator.

If the risk and impact analysis and the planning of the implementation are acceptable, the change manager determines the approval that is required for the change. Approval is required from the representatives of customers who will be affected by the change implementation if the implementation causes either of the following conditions:

A service is unavailable or degraded during service hours

The functionality of a service changes

If these conditions do not apply to the planned implementation, approval from the service owner is sufficient.

If the change is rejected by an approver, the change manager finds the reason. If appropriate, the change manager asks the change coordinator to perform additional risk and impact analysis or to adjust the planning.

If the change was rejected for any other reason, the change manager informs the change coordinator that the change cannot be implemented.
